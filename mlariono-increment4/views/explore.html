Our Mission

MonoMuse is dedicated to bridging the gap between art and technology, creating a space where human creativity and digital innovation meet. We believe that art is not confined to canvas and marble — it lives in code, circuits, and the spaces in between. Our mission is to make that intersection visible, accessible, and inspiring to all.

Who We Serve

MonoMuse welcomes a diverse community of visitors, from curious newcomers to seasoned art enthusiasts and technologists. Our programs and exhibitions are designed for:

  • Students and educators exploring creative technology in the classroom
  • Artists and designers seeking inspiration from emerging digital tools
  • Tech professionals interested in the cultural dimensions of their work
  • Families and young visitors discovering art and science together
  • Community members looking for meaningful cultural experiences

Our Story

Founded in 2005, MonoMuse began as a small gallery space with a single vision: to show that technology could be a medium for profound artistic expression. Over the past two decades, we have grown into one of the most recognized contemporary museums at the intersection of art and digital culture.

Milestones

  • 2005 — MonoMuse founded with its inaugural exhibition, "Signal and Noise"
  • 2008 — Expanded to a permanent 30,000 sq ft facility in the arts district
  • 2011 — Launched the Annual Digital Arts Fellowship program
  • 2014 — Welcomed our 500,000th visitor and opened the interactive lab wing
  • 2017 — Introduced rotating international exhibitions in partnership with global museums
  • 2020 — Launched MonoMuse Online, bringing virtual exhibitions to global audiences
  • 2023 — Celebrated 18 years with the landmark "Future/Past" retrospective exhibition
