Current Exhibitions

Digital Horizons

An immersive exploration of how digital tools are reshaping the boundaries of visual art. Featuring works by 14 international artists, Digital Horizons presents large-scale projections, generative graphics, and sensor-driven environments that respond to visitor presence. On display through August 2026.

Code and Canvas

A meditation on the relationship between algorithm and aesthetics. This exhibition pairs hand-crafted paintings with their computationally generated counterparts, asking visitors to consider what it means to create when the creator is a machine. Running through July 2026.

Human Signal

An interactive sculpture installation exploring human communication in the digital age. Visitors become part of the artwork — their movement, voice, and biometric data are woven into a live-evolving soundscape and visual display. Permanent collection.

Coming Soon

After the Algorithm — Opening September 2026

A survey of post-AI creative practices, featuring artists who have chosen to reject, subvert, or collaborate with artificial intelligence in their work.
